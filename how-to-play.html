How to Play

Rules and controls for all puzzle games

🎯
CHROMINO

A sparse board is scattered with colored dots. Your job is to cover every dot using tetromino shapes — but a shape cannot land on a dot of its own color.

A blue T-shape covers a red dot — valid!
A blue shape cannot land on a blue dot.
1
Select a shape from the panel on the right (or below on mobile). It highlights when selected.
2
Rotate the shape using the ↻ button or press R. Hover over the board to preview placement.
3
Click (or drag) the shape onto the board. The preview turns red if placement is invalid.
4
Cover all dots to complete the level. Shapes can overlap empty cells — only the dot rule matters.
The one rule: a shape may not cover a dot of the same color. It can cover dots of any other color freely.

🧩
TESSERA

Every cell on the board is filled with a colored dot. Tile the entire board with tetromino shapes leaving no gaps — but a shape cannot sit on a dot of its own color.

The board is fully tiled. Each shape covers dots of other colors.
Notice no cell is left empty and no shape sits on its own color.
1
Select a shape and rotate it with ↻ or R to find an orientation that fits.
2
Place it on the board. It must cover only dots of different colors. No cell can be left uncovered.
3
Place all shapes to tile the board completely with no gaps or overlaps.
Key difference from Chromino: in Tessera the board must be completely tiled — every cell covered. In Chromino, shapes can hang over empty space as long as all dots are covered.

COLORKU

A colorful take on Sudoku. Fill every cell of the grid so that each color appears exactly once in every row and column. Larger grids also add box constraints.

Solved 3×3
4×4 in progress
Square cells = given clues (fixed).
Round cells = your answers.
Shading shows the 2×2 boxes.
Conflicts glow red automatically.
1
Tap a color swatch at the bottom to select it. It gets a white ring when active.
2
Tap any empty cell to fill it with the selected color. Tap the same cell again to clear it.
3
Conflicts — duplicate colors in a row, column or box — highlight in red instantly.
4
Fill every cell with no conflicts to solve the puzzle.
Levels 1–2: 3×3 grid, 3 colors, rows & columns only.
Levels 3–4: 4×4 grid, 4 colors, plus 2×2 boxes.
Levels 5–6: 6×6 grid, 6 colors, plus 2×3 boxes.

OUTLINES

A country silhouette appears on the world map. Type its name before the timer runs out. How many countries do you know?

1
A highlighted country outline appears on the map. Type your guess in the text box.
2
Press Enter to submit. Correct guesses reveal the country name and score points.
3
Use Skip to pass a country you don't know. Skipped countries don't count against you.
4
Progress through all countries — the map fills in as you identify each one.
Tip: common abbreviations and alternate spellings are accepted (e.g. "USA", "UK", "Czechia" or "Czech Republic").

WORD SEARCH

A grid of letters hides a set of words. Find them all — words can run in any of eight directions: across, down, or diagonal, forwards or backwards.

1
Scan the grid for the first and last letter of a word on the list to the right.
2
Click the starting letter, then click the ending letter to highlight a word. On mobile, tap and drag across the letters.
3
Correct words light up and are crossed off the list. Find all words to complete the puzzle.
Directions: horizontal, vertical, and all four diagonals — each word can run either forwards or backwards along its line.
Play Word Search →

MORSE CODE

Listen to a Morse code sequence and decode what letter or word is being transmitted. A glowing lamp flashes the signal — can you read it?

· − − −
Easy mode shows the dots and dashes
1
Press Play to flash the lamp. Watch the pattern of short (·) and long (−) flashes.
2
Use the reference table at the bottom to look up the letter for each symbol sequence.
3
Type your answer and press Check. Decode every letter in the word to advance.
4
Switch to Hard mode once you know the alphabet — no dot/dash trace, just the lamp.
Easy mode: dot/dash symbols appear as the lamp flashes — great for learning.
Hard mode: only the lamp is shown — tests your real Morse recall.
Speed: choose Slow / Normal / Fast to adjust the flash rate.

1
